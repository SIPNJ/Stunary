Instruction Box

General Instruction

Basic Operator

Plus

To plus, you need to click the plus sign (+) button and the display will show the plus symbol.

Minus

To subtract, you need to click the minus sign (-) button and the display will show the minus symbol.

Multiply

To multiply, you need to click the multiply sign (×) button and the display will show the multiply symbol.

Minus

To divide, you need to click the divide sign (÷) button and the display will show the divide symbol.

Super Operator

Power

To power, you need to click the power sign (^) button and the display will show the power symbol.

Also, if you want to square, just click (^2) button and the display will show.

Rememner these one: Shift + ^ = % Alpha + ^ = ‰

Square root

To square root, you need to click the square root sign (√) button and the display will show the plus symbol.

Logarithm

Simple Log button is decimal logarithm.

Ln button is Napierian logarithm or natural lagarithm.

Loga button is logarithm with random base, it should be typed as Loga(a,b) where a is base and b is anti-logarithm.

Variable

To assign number to variable, click Shift and number button from 1 to 9 or just simply click only M+ button.

To recall variable, click Alpha and a number button from 1 to 9 or M+ button.

Remember these one:

1 : A
2 : B
3 : C
4 : D
5 : E
6 : F
7 : G
8 : H
9 : I

GCD and LCM

GCD is Greatest Common Divisor, it is also known as Highest Common Factor - LCF

LCM is Least Common Multiple

Remember these one: Alpha + x = GCD Alpha + ÷ = LCM

Note: GCD and LCM is just for 2 numbers, for 3 or more mubers, we should type as GCD(a,GCD(b,c)) or LCM(a,LCM(b,LCM(c,LCM(d,e))))

Trigonometry Operator

Basic Trigonometry

There are 3 trigonometry operators that are the most basic. They are Sine, Cosine and Tangent

Simply, you can see and click these buttons in normal mode to display those one, but remember to type ")" after any function.

Inverse Trigonometry

If the ratio is not enough and you want an inverse function to determine the angle, you should click on Shift Button to have an inverse function; after that, you click which trigonometry you want to inverse.

More further

If you want to calculate reciprocal function, you can click on Alpha Button then one of three basic trigonometry.

Of course these one have their inverse function, which means you can click Alpha Button + Shift Button to have an inverse reciprocal trigonometry.

Hyperbolic Trigonometry

For a higher class, we must use hyperbolic trigonometry which means you click HYP Button and other basic trigonometry.

If you want reciprocal, just add more Alpha Button

If you want inverse, just add more Shift Button

If you want inverse reciprocal hyperbolic trigonometry operators, just simply click HYP Button + Alpha Button + Shift Button
